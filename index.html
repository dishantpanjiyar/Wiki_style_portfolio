Dishant Panjiyar is a full-stack software developer and freelance consultant based in Kathmandu, Nepal. A recent graduate with a BSc (Hons) in Computing, he works primarily on web application development, spanning both front-end and back-end systems, and has delivered enterprise software including point-of-sale and inventory management systems for small and medium businesses.[1]

Beyond development, Panjiyar has experience in client handling, social media management, and graphic design, and has served as project manager on several of his own projects, allocating and dividing tasks across teams.[2] He has also experimented with machine learning, training a movie recommendation and rating model on user reviews and critic scores.

Early life and educationedit

Panjiyar was born in Bardaghat, Nawalparasi, Nepal. He completed his secondary education at Nepal APF Secondary School in Kirtipur, Kathmandu, followed by his +2 (higher secondary) studies at GoldenGate International College in Old Baneshwor, Kathmandu.[3] He went on to earn a BSc (Hons) in Computing from Islington College in Kamalpokhari, Kathmandu, graduating as a recent graduate of the program.

Careeredit

After completing an internship — name of the company/organization where you interned? — Panjiyar began working part-time as a freelance developer, taking on full-stack web development projects for clients across several industries.[1] His freelance work has included e-commerce websites, transit-mapping tools, and management systems for restaurants, cafes, clinics, labs, and pharmacies.

Notable projectsedit

APD — Auto Parts Dealership Platform

A full dealership management system built around an ASP.NET Core Web API backend and a React/Vite frontend, using PostgreSQL through Entity Framework Core.[1] The backend covers dependency injection and EF Core patterns such as eager loading, soft delete, and DTO mapping, alongside async data flows and CORS configuration. The frontend implements a master-detail fetch pattern with optimistic UI updates, Base64 image uploads, and form validation via react-hook-form and Zod.

KTM Transit

A progressive web app that helps riders plan bus journeys across the Kathmandu Valley, developed for Islington College.[1] The app functions offline using IndexedDB and a service worker, renders maps with Leaflet.js and OpenStreetMap data, geocodes locations with Nominatim, and calculates routes with OSRM, backed by Supabase for data storage and authentication.

Applied math modeling

Fitted exponential growth models to Nepal's GDP (World Bank, 2000–2023) and population (1960–2023) figures, evaluating fit quality using R² values and documenting interpolation, extrapolation, and the limitations of each model. A related project solved a supply-and-demand equilibrium problem both algebraically and through an interactive visualization.

Other projects

Panjiyar has also built management systems for cafes and restaurants (sales, billing, table booking, inventory, and vendor orders), a lab and pharmacy management system (vendor sales, profit/loss tracking, medicine inventory, patient card booking, and digital patient records), an e-commerce website, and a movie recommendation model trained on user reviews and critic ratings.
